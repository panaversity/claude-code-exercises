# 15 Scenarios: Skill or Prompt?

Read each scenario and decide: should you build a skill, or just write a prompt?

## Scenario 1: Weekly Team Email
Every Monday, you write an email to your team summarizing last week's progress and this week's priorities. The format is always the same: 3 bullet highlights, 3 priorities, and a motivational closing line.

## Scenario 2: Translating a Poem
Your friend wrote a poem in Urdu and wants you to help translate it to English while preserving the emotional tone. This is a one-time request.

## Scenario 3: Student Feedback Reports
You're a teacher who writes feedback for 30 students every month. Each report follows the same structure: strengths, areas for improvement, specific examples, and a personalized encouragement note. You want the tone to be warm but honest.

## Scenario 4: Naming a Product
Your startup needs a name for a new feature. You want Claude to brainstorm 20 options.

## Scenario 5: Invoice Processing
Your small business receives 20-30 invoices per month in various formats (PDF, email text, photos). You need to extract: vendor name, date, amount, category, and payment due date, then add them to a spreadsheet.

## Scenario 6: Explaining a Concept
A student asks you to explain how neural networks work in simple terms.

## Scenario 7: Code Review Comments
You review pull requests daily and always follow the same pattern: check for security issues, performance concerns, code style, test coverage, and documentation. You want consistent, constructive comments.

## Scenario 8: Party Planning
You're planning your friend's birthday party. You need ideas for venue, food, and entertainment.

## Scenario 9: Social Media Posts
Your company posts on LinkedIn 3 times per week. Every post follows brand guidelines: specific tone of voice, approved hashtags, character limits, and must include a call to action. Different team members write posts but quality should be consistent.

## Scenario 10: Debugging an Error
You got a weird Python error and want Claude to help you fix it.

## Scenario 11: Customer Response Templates
Your support team handles 50+ customer emails daily. Most fall into 8 categories (refund request, technical issue, billing question, etc.). Each category has a different response structure and tone, but personalization is needed for each customer.

## Scenario 12: Summarizing a Book
You want Claude to summarize "Thinking, Fast and Slow" for your book club discussion.

## Scenario 13: Daily Standup Notes
Every day, you convert your messy standup notes into a formatted Slack message. It always has: what you did yesterday, what you're doing today, and blockers. The Slack channel expects a specific emoji format.

## Scenario 14: Competitive Analysis
Once per quarter, you research 5 competitors and produce a comparison matrix using the same 12 criteria. The format, sources, and analysis framework are consistent every quarter.

## Scenario 15: Writing a Cover Letter
You're applying for one specific job and need a tailored cover letter.
